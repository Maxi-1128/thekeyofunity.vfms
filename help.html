FAQ — The Key of Unity

> What is The Key of Unity about in one sentence?
A magical artifact that helps Hogwarts, forcing students to work together to save the school.


> Where does the story take place?
Primarily at Hogwarts and a hidden chamber.


> When in the Harry Potter timeline is it set?
During the school year while Harry and friends are students 1991-1998 (canon side story).


> What is the central conflict of the play?
Whether students and staff can unite to control the Key before Voldemort uses it to divide the houses.


> How does the Key of Unity work?
It is used to activate a stone located in the hidden chamber, or it can be used to destroy the stone. Once the stone is activated, it will create a magical boundary around Hogwarts that even the darkest curses cannot destroy.


> Who created the Key of Unity?
Legend says the Hogwarts Founders forged it together as a final test of unity and a defense for Hogwarts.


> Does the play follow book canon exactly?
No—it's a respectful, unofficial fan story that fits alongside canon without replacing it.


> Are there any original characters?
Yes - All characters are original (created by jkr)


> Is there a villain?
The main “antagonist” is division itself; some characters oppose unity for personal reasons. Lord Voldemort opposes it to take over Hogwarts.


> What message does the story try to give?
Courage matters, but unity wins.

> Which character changes the most over the play?

> What does the Key of Unity look like?
A palm-sized, golden key that glows when the stone is activated.


> Are magical effects practical or digital?
Mostly practical with light, sound, and choreography; minimal projections.


> Is the Forbidden Forest included?
Yes! It is a spot of dueling.


> How are spells represented on stage?
Light pulses from wands, timed sound cues, and ensemble movement.


> Are animals or magical creatures shown?
Not physically, but implied through sound and shadow effects rather than puppets and real animals.
